Onekama students 'take pride in' new esports club

'It takes a total team to pull it all together'

Written By: Kishore Kumar
New Delhi
Published: March 28, 2025 6:24 IST

ONEKAMA — Some students head home after a long day of school, grab a controller and unwind by playing their favorite video game. Soon, students at Onekama Consolidated Schools will be using their gaming skills to represent their school as the district launched its esports club this school year.

The club is comprised of 24 students in grades six through 12. "We've been practicing every couple weeks and really just getting our feet wet into what this is all about — the competition part, the equipment — and kids are excited," said superintendent Dan Mesyar. "... The nice thing about this is you could do esports with two or three students, or you could do it with 50. ... It's a place they can come in after school, compete and have fun." Mesyar said the idea for an esports club came from a student survey. "We gave surveys out to all students last year that asked, 'What are some things you'd like to see?' Fishing club was one and esports was two," he said. "... When we say 'your voice matters,' those are two things that happened. ... They said, 'Hey, we'd like these opportunities,' and we're able to pull it off."

Mesyar said the club was in its "infant stages" in the fall.
"We did surveys — What games do you like? What do you like to play? And then (we looked at) what ones are offered for esports competitions in the state of Michigan," he said. "... It's one thing to just game and have fun doing it with your friends, and that's cool, but that's not what we're doing here. We're trying to (focus on) the competitive esports-sanctioned games in the state."

"'Mario Kart' is one that we're really starting to get pretty good at," Mesyar said. "... There are three or four (games) that rose to the top with what (the students) rated them. Then we're probably going to really start practicing those after spring break and do some scrimmaging. Then next fall, hey, it's on."

Mesyar said he was involved with the formation of an esports program at Ludington Area Schools, so he more or less fell into the coaching role at Onekama. Fortunately, he said, he's had a lot of help. "Thank goodness for Yvonne Barberini, my administrative assistant — she's been huge as far as helping out. Alex Wolkow, our tech director here, he's been huge," Mesyar said. "We're kind of tri-coaches, really. It takes a total team to pull it all together when you talk about getting in here, getting food, getting set, getting everything hooked up. That's what it really takes to make this happen."
